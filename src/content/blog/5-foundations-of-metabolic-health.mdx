Metabolic health isn't a single lab value or a magic supplement. It's how efficiently your body converts food into usable energy — and how well it recovers from the stress of modern life. The good news: small, consistent habits compound into meaningful change. Here are five foundations we return to with nearly every client.

**1. Protein at every meal.** Adequate protein supports lean muscle, satiety, and stable blood sugar. Aim for a palm-sized portion — more if you're training hard.

**2. Fiber before refined carbs.** Vegetables, legumes, and whole grains slow glucose absorption and feed a healthier gut microbiome. Build your plate around plants first.

**3. Movement after meals.** A 10–15 minute walk after eating improves glucose clearance more than you'd expect. It's one of the simplest metabolic tools available.

**4. Sleep as a metabolic input.** Poor sleep raises cortisol and insulin resistance. Protecting 7–8 hours isn't a luxury — it's foundational lab work for your hormones.

**5. Data when it helps, not when it distracts.** CGM trials, periodic labs, and wearable trends can reveal patterns your body can't articulate. Use them to refine — not obsess.
